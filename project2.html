Project Title: Digital Scaffolding for Financial Literacy | Capitec Speculative Case Study

The Challenge

For non-bank customers, applying for credit can be a high-anxiety process filled with information overload and complex compliance requirements. In a fast-paced retail banking environment like Capitec's, learners (the customers) need a support system that reduces cognitive load and prevents application errors before they reach the branch.

The Solution

I designed a high-fidelity, 8-step visual job aid using Canva, aimed at simplifying the credit acquisition journey. The project serves as a bridge for the learner, moving them from passive interest to active, prepared application through clear Digital Scaffolding.

Instructional Design Strategy

  • Bloom's Taxonomy Alignment: The aid is structured to move the user from Understanding (eligibility and requirements) to Applying (navigating the estimate tool and branch visit) and finally Evaluating (selecting a personalized offer).
  • Cognitive Load Management: Information is chunked into manageable steps to prevent learner burnout. Each step uses a clear action title to maintain momentum.
  • Visual Hierarchy: Utilizing Capitec's brand identity (Navy Blue and Red), I prioritized high-contrast headings and iconography to ensure the guide is scannable in under 10 seconds, a critical requirement for mobile-first users.
  • User-Centric Tone: The language was translated from Bank-Speak into plain, accessible English to align with Capitec's core brand value of simplicity.

Tools Used

  • Canva: For graphic layout and brand-aligned visual design.
  • Instructional Design Principles: Scaffolding, Bloom's Taxonomy, and Minimalism.
